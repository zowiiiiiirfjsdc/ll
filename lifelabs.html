First, lets have a recap of our problem and solution from quarter 1 and

Wife-selling

Wife-seling is an act that involves a husband auctioning off and selling
their wives for not only money, but also as a way of divorce between
the seller-husband and the wife. The buyer on the other hand gets
married to the wife that is being sold. This was a popular method of
self divorce and self marriage during the 17th century in England.

Solution

Our chosen solutino is: "Challenge/debate their ideas and stereotypes
about women by protesting about the ethics of wife-selling and flaws".
We all decided that this would be the best, most impactful and realistic
solution that couldve helped solving our chosen problem. Even though
the other solutions stated on the mind map wouldve also helped with
putting a halt to wife selling, not all of them are exactly realistic and
applicable to the timeline where our problem happened. Our group
made sure to apply all the possible solutions in the timeline, and came
to conclusion that our chosen one would be the best to not only raise
awareness, but also let peoples voices be heard. Why did we choose
this solution out of all the various ones we brainstormed? It is because
of these factors:

Education

We can all agree that education is one of the most important and crucial
factors that shapes us up, making us what we are now. Because of the
patriarcy and multiple misogynistic gender stereotypes during the 17th
century, women did not have any proper access to education.

Empowerment

Without the rights to education, empowerment and independence played
a crucial role during the 17th century to these women in order to survive.
Showing support can lead to encouragement and empowerment to one
another.

Spreading information

Women bringing awareness to eachouther about the dehumanizing truth
about wife selling. Making the government hear women's voices is a good
way to make them acknowledge their discrimination against women and for
the government to start having laws to protect women. Fighting for their
rights can show that women are more capable than the mindset everyone
had towards them. Having access to education without discrimination, can
lead to empowered women and people who can solve the problems of
their marriage without having to sell anyone. Making people aware with
being human and leading them to disagreeing with selling people, will
decrease the acts of wife-selling and the support for it.

Usability test

After making our preliminary prototype, we now move on to
the second step, which is the usability test. This usability test
is being conducted in order to gain our taret audiences feed
back about our prototype, to ensure an effective outcome.

Questions:

- On a scale of 1 to 5, how much of an impact do you think our
poster would have in real-life situations?
- Could our poster cause any negative implications?
- What additional features can we add to make our poster better?
- Did the layout of our poster have any negative effects on the
way you viewed the information stated?
- What did you like about our poster?
- What did you dislike about our poster?
- What are your thoughts about the layout and visual elements of
our poster?
- How would you rate the organization and structure of content
within the poster?
